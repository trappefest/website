Location

Our primary location is a gorgeous spot, set on the edge of a swimmable reservoir.
The event is a wild camping set up (no facilities nor toilets... Good ol' shovel!)
It isn't a bookable location so we won't know if we can secure the spot until a few days before the event, when some of us get there as early as possible after Christmas.
If we can't get that spot, we have a plan B in the Grampians.
The final location will be shared as soon as we know, a few days before the event.
But let's plan for the best: Bring your sunnies, sun screen and bather, pitch your tent amongst the gum trees and bring your best festival outfit for a day and night to remember!

Stay tuned for the exact location!

FAQs

The essentials:
  • water (plenty, think like 4L/person/day)
  • sun protection (sun screen, sunnies, hat, umbrellas/tarp, etc.)
  • food and cooking equipment (in case of a fire ban we may not be allowed to make a camp fire)
  • reusable cup (preferably with a lid, it will help keeping your drink free from dust and insects)
  • tent, mattress and sleeping bag
  • bathers and towel
  • eco-friendly soap (for shower and dishes)
  • toilet paper
  • head torch
  • insect repellant
  • hydrolites
  • your smile, good vibes and respect
Nice to have:
  • chair and table
  • hamac and picnic blanket
  • esky and ice
  • solar shower
  • outdoor games
  • fairy lights, onsie and other festival attire
Nope. We're camping in the bush. There's no toilet, electricity nor bins onsite. We'll have shovels with us to dig holes for numbers 2s. All rubbish will need to be taken with us. No littering policy, let's leave the place as clean as we find it.
If we get the spot we want, Halls Gap is about 40 minutes drive away from camp. You'll find all the supplies you might need there, but keep in mind that most shops may be closed over NYE.
Again, if we get the spot we want, yes a 2wd can easily access it. If we move to another spot that requires a 4wd to access, we'll organise shuttle runs with our 4wds to bring you there from where you have to leave your car (as we did last year).
